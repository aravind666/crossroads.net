Justice: Ways to Engage

You can choose from four primary ways to engage in Justice work at Crossroads.

Take a look at the categories below, and then sign up for the one that best matches your skills and interest (don't worry, you're not commiting to 50 hours a week). Once you sign up, you’ll be contacted by a leader (within a few days) who'll give you more direction. The leader will explain our various initiatives, such as End Slavery Cincinnati, the Cincinnati Human Trafficking Report, our IJM partnership in Mumbai and others, and explore opportunties for you to work on assignments on an as-needed basis.

Research

We need folks who can research and distill their learning down into a readable set of facts.  Opportunities include investigating a particular topic for an event, drafting a briefing paper on best practices or writing a grant.

Plan

Plan a project or an event. All of our initiatives require various levels of planning (or project management), from mapping out event strategy to putting together the logistics of a care package drive.

Pray

Pray for our work and help others pray. Prayer is at the core of how we engage in justice, because the work of our hands matters little unless we make God the center of how we’re engaging. From distributing requests from partner organizations to organizing prayer experiences, prayer is central to our justice work.

Communicate

Share information in creative, concise, and challenging ways. Use your gift to share the reality of God’s heart toward the oppressed with others, so their heart can be transformed.  Whether you enjoy writing, teaching or photography, we need your help to communicate through words, film, music and images.

See something that piques your interest? Just click on the team you'd like to join to sign up. We'll follow up with details.

You can also learn more about the global battle for justice by checking out these resources and by signing up to receive updates from Crossroads’ Justice Team.
