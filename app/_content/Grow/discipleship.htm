Discipleship

Resources for maturing and multiplying

 

Jesus’ last command on earth was “go make disciples.” It’s a simple ask, but not necessarily an easy one. What’s a disciple? How do I make them? Where do I start? This page holds our latest ideas on discipleship at Crossroads—resources, ways to get connected, and whatever else we can give you to set you up for success.

 

Discipleship at Crossroads

A disciple is someone who looks like Jesus in both character and competency. The way He acted, we’re acting. The way he loved, we love. The way he brought freedom, we do, too. Crossroads is running after a discipling culture—one that’s bursting with disciple-making disciples who know how to introduce people to Jesus. Our two primary ways of growing disciples are Huddles and GO Groups. Huddles are a group of 4-6 people committed to being trained as disciple-making disciples. GO Groups are communities of people using their spheres of influence to introduce others to God.
