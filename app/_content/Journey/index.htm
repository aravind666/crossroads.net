What is a Journey?

 

A community-wide Journey, this year called Kingdom Come, focuses the entire church on one topic for six weeks. Everyone—from kids to the old folks—is on the same page, learning about the same topics and growing in similar ways. A Journey consists of three parts: the weekend message, small group interaction and individual experiments. For the six weeks of the journey—beginning October 12—it is the main thing. Everything else shuts down. Everyone participates. Everyone is challenged.

Get in a group 

The small group experience is an important part of the Journey. Here are some ways you can find a group:

 

-Click here to search for a group. You can specify the kind of group you’re looking for, when it meets and the area of town closest to you.

-Ask friends or people you serve with and create your own group. (One of you will need to sign up as a host.)

-Join an onsite group, open to anyone and meets one evening during the week at all three sites.

-Attend a Connect Event to meet others looking for a group.

 

Host a Group

If you need more info about hosting a Journey small group, check out this page.

 

-First, sign up as a host. By signing up, this gives us your basic contact info, so we can send you details and info specifically geared for hosts. Click here to sign up.

-Second, you have the option to post your group, letting you advertise your small group to other journey participants—whether you’re starting a new group from scratch, or adding people to your existing group. Click here to Post a Group.

-If you need to edit or delete your group post, go back to www.crossroads.net, login, then click Grow>Small Groups>Find A Small Group>Zip Code Input. Enter your zip code, find your group posting, then edit/delete.
