Friday, August 12

Whom Will You Serve?

God, remind me of the ways youve been faithful in my life. Thank you for keeping your promises.

READ

Joshua 24:8-15

REFLECT

Throughout his life, God asked a lot of Joshua. He asked him to lead the Israelites across the Jordan River and through many battles for the Promised Land. Joshua always obeyed, and God always kept his promises.

As he approached the end of his life, Joshua continued a life-long pattern of speaking boldly to the people. This time, he presented them with a simple challenge, "Whom will you serve?"

We all make decisions. Some are more critical than others. But the reality is our choices point to whats most important to us. In some cases, maybe even our "idols." Like Bob Dylan said, "It may be the devil, or it may be the Lord, but youre gonna have to serve somebody."

RESPOND

As you go through your day, consider each choice you make no matter how small. Ask God to show you the idols in your life, and who or what you are serving. It may be yourself, or your money, or your Facebook profile. Jesus said that no one can serve two masters. Ask Gods forgiveness for any idols in your life. Make a new covenant with Him that "you and your house will serve the Lord."

WEEK TWO DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

The Friday edition of The Daily features discussion questions based on the weeks content. These questions are meant to spark conversation with your spouse, your roommate, or a small group of friends who also subscribe to The Daily.

  • What distracts you from staying focused on God and his will for your life? Maybe its unhealthy relationships, selfish interests or a need to be in control? Take turns sharing these distractions with the group.
  • This week we saw much evidence of God keeping his promises. How do you feel about Gods promises in your life? Do you have faith that He will keep them? Or do you doubt? Take turns confessing any fears or doubts. And as a group, ask God to honor your confessions and build your faith.
  • What role does worship play in your daily life? The Bible shows us that it can be super powerful. As a group, take some time to worship together. There are many ways to worship. You can read aloud some passages from the book of Psalms, or sing along to a worship cd together. Or simply spend some time in prayer together reminding God of his greatness and all the wonderful things he has done in your life.
