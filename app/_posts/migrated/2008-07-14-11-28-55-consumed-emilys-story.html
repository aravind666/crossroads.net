I used to be a materialistic brat. I always had the latest and greatest fashions, and I loved the way people responded positively to how I looked. I grew up getting material rewards that my parents told me I “deserved.”

God took me on the Consumed journey to address the issues that ultimately changed my life: Buying things doesn’t bring me happiness. I don’t need stuff to have friendships or to have a sense of self worth. Racking up a tab at J.Crew and Banana Republic was a feeble attempt at filling an emotional vacancy.

I’m not debt-free by any means, but I am free of the emotional baggage and bondage that held me for so long. I have a sense of peace that comes from understanding I was created with a sense of purpose. Doing things—like building homes with GO New Orleans—are more important than buying
things. I have a sense of contentment I had never had from continually buying things.

Unraveling 29 years of materialism is not easy, but I’m on a path toward debt reduction. Don’t get me wrong; when Banana has a sale, I do struggle. But I realize know that I want to store my treasures in heaven, not in my closet.
