The Work Begins.

After two days of easing into life in Mumbai, the sun broke beautiful this morning as we grabbed breakfast and huddled together to pray and worship before our first day of "work." While Brandon will provide some play by play of our day, Id rather focus on some color commentary on the team in general.


Crossroads, you have an outstanding group of representatives on the ground here in Mumbai. I couldnt be more pleased with how people have adapted to the environment, pitched in to help, and havent grumbled a bit. Theyve truly been a joy to work alongside, and Im thankful theyve made the role so easy. Today at the conclusion of our time at the aftercare home the superintendent made a special point to publicly thank this group for the way they integrated with the girls, and genuinely connected in the time they were given. Theyre a blessing over here, without a doubt.

On our way to the home I spent time with the founder of Crossover, Keith Desouza. Keith is a tremendous man, and its an honor to know and serve underneath him. He was telling our group of a recent rescue another partner (IJM) had just performed. Friends, the God we serve is bigger, more powerful, and more awesome than we could ever imagine. He is the best aftercare provider ever.

Good night friends.

Its been a wonderful day (as detailed by Brandon below).

- Andrew

From Brandon:
Where to begin, oh where to begin. Days like today are difficult to put into words, but for right now, Ill do my best to convey the group experience. Starting off our morning, after breakfast and plenty of coffee, the group all converged in our hotels beautiful conference room, where we were led in prayer and worship by the wonderful and amazing Andy Reider. Through song, our emotions were put through the test as we bowed our heads and prayed aloud as the Holy Spirit swept through the room. I dont know if I haveeverfelt as full or enriched with the Holy Spirit more than when Andy sang this morning with the group backing him up. We took our time with any individual prayers that came to mind and dismissed off to the cars on an extremely high note. Inspiring and powerful would be an understatement.


Afterwards, charged up and ready to take on the world, our group headed out for a long drive to our first aftercare home, stopping along the way for lunch at a wonderful restaurant that highly expanded our flavor palates. This was our first real chance to eat with our hands and get a little dirty with our food, and every serving (there were quite a few) was pretty delicious.


After lunch, charged and fed, we were ready for our first home visit, nerves ran high for all as we drove down the dusty roads and took off our shoes at the doorstep before entering. Gathered in what might be considered the living room, the girls soon entered, some with shy faces and apprehension, others with beaming smiles and warm welcomes. Though some girls were clearly farther along in their recovery process than others, we all sat down in small groups of 3-4 to make some crafts and converse as much as we could. Personally, as a man entering this home, my expectations on what was to come, as far as interactions go, were low. These girls have been through more than any person ever should, and if they wanted to completely reject and ignore us, what more could any of us guys expect? However, in our groups, though some were shy, the smiles started to come out, and the shells began to break for almost everyone. In my personal small group, myself, Paula and Tiana, had the pleasure of two wonderful girls to interact with. Our first girl, I shall call her Mary, barely spoke any English at all, and was at first very apprehensive to the three of us, even though we had some staff around to translate, then we had another girl join the group. Well call her Sarah. Sarah had one of the mostcontagioussmiles I have ever seen, and with a little bit of joking about my mispronunciation of her name, we made personal journals and flower pens, even Mary began to enjoy herself. Though I could never mention to the girls personally how beautiful they are, I felt it on my heart that they were truly a blessing to be with us. As I stole a glance at the scars, tattoos, burns and marks on the girls arms, I could not describe the emotions that flashed through my mind as I played along with them and watch them open up to a room full of strange Americans.


Once done with crafts, the girls had an area set up with crafts and bags they had personally made, and the whole group was given the pleasure of shopping through the items and purchasing anything that stood out. To see the faces as they counted the wads of cash we handed over, just knowing that what they had made had a sense of value to someone, was something that will stick in my mind forever. Im honored to say that both Mary and Sarah are excellent with their math skills and money exchange and handled most of the transfers for the whole group. I knew them for an hour, and I was already proud.


After shopping we still werent done, and were treated to a group dance that several of the girls lead. To see us funny Americans try to dance along with them gave them much pleasure and plenty to talk about in their home language. As a special treat to them, a male dance team soon assembled (with myself included), led and choreographed by Mike, we all used our best moves and provided memories for the onlookers of both nations. The dance was capped off by Mike doing the worm across the marble floors, to the applause and shouts of all.


Today is a day that I will never forget for the rest of my life. I am confident in saying that every person on this trip has been changed for the better as a result of the short time we spent with these young girls, and we can only hope that they were able to take some small pleasure away from having us. We drove away this evening, as the girls leaned over the fence and waved goodbyes, wishing we could stay. Before we stepped out the door, Mary stood up in front of everyone in the room, and through a translator, made a declaration about her new American friends. She acknowledged how far we had traveled, and gave us a warm thanks for all we had done for them. She said we gave them a day of pleasure and many memories, and I cant help but think, than none of us will forget those girls and all they have shown us.

Loving Mumbai,

Brandon Rose
