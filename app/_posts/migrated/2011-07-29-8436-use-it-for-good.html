Use It For Good

God, keep my heart pure and my tongue humble.

READ

Ephesians 4:29-32

REFLECT

Ah, the great and mighty tongue. Made up of sixteen little muscles, its celebrated as the one of the strongest, most versatile organs in our body. We know that its power is great and can change the course of lives around us by simply speaking words. Left to its own nature, though, the tongue will choose evil, speaking lies and spiritual death. But with the guidance of the Holy Spirit, the tongue can speak truth and love.

Remember, we can try our best to fake it, but the tongue will inevitably show our true characters. God wants to clean the junk out of our hearts, so that speaking forgiveness and compassion comes naturally, like fruit. And before we know it, Super Tongue is using its power for good.

RESPOND

While "Sticks and Stones" was a lousy childhood lesson, "think before you speak" was actually quite brilliant. Even better, lets try asking before we speak. Today, and as long as you have a tongue, practice asking God for his input before you open your mouth and unleash that wild tongue.

WEEK TWO DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

The Friday edition of The Daily features discussion questions based on the weeks content. These questions are meant to spark conversation with your spouse, your roommate, or a small group of friends who also subscribe to The Daily.

Do you have a testimony? Have you written it down or shared it aloud with others? Why or why not? Each person take turns sharing their God story for five minutes. Use a timer. Dont worry about how it sounds, just practice telling your story.

  • Consider how you use words in a typical day. Do you talk a lot, or a little? Why? Whats more challenging for you, growing in the ability to speak, or growing in the ability to be silent?
  • Youve had many assignments over the past two weeks that have required you to speak aloud. Take turns sharing what youve learned and experienced as a result of speaking aloud.
  • Take a few minutes and practice praying out loud. Each person pray for the person on their right. Dont worry about what to say or how to say it. Just speak, and God will use your words to speak life.
