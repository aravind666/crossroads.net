In our efforts to bless the people of Mamelodi, we hope to leverage the goodwill of other partners who can bring much needed expertise and resources to the table. We have had many fruitful discussions with universities and medical schools, international and South African non-profits and corporations.

 

One of the most encouraging relationships we’ve built is with Luxottica corporation’s global non-profit, OneSight. Headquartered just down the street from our Mason site, OneSight runs free eye clinics all over the world, giving out thousands of free eyeglasses and sunglasses in the process.

 

Over the next two weeks, OneSight, Charity & Faith (our partner church) and Crossroads are working together to run free eye clinics in Mamelodi for over 10,000 people!  A team of eye doctors and support people from around the world (including 15 from Crossroads) are now on their way to South Africa, while an army of Charity & Faith volunteers are going door to door in some of the toughest neighborhoods of Mamelodi to hand out tickets for the clinics, often by candlelight. In an area of South Africa with little access to eye care, these clinics will offer to many their first opportunity to have their vision checked by an eye doctor, potentially identifying and thwarting blindness-causing diseases, or allowing a middle-aged Mamelodian the chance to read a book or drive a car for the first time since his/her eyesight went bad years ago.
