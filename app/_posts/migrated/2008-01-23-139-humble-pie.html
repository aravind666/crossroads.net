--- layout: post title: "Humble Pie" subtitle: "" date: 2008-01-23 category: "Crossroads" tag: - crossroads comments: true created-by: "Todd" --- I've been thinking a lot over the past few days about Chuck Mingo's talk on humility. I have rarely thought of humility as a conscious choice. Instead, I've often thought of it more as something that you either have or you don't.

In doing some reading this week, I came across a verse in the book of Daniel in the bible. It describes a scene in which an angel visits Daniel to give him direction and a vision for what's about to happen. One particular thing the angel said struck me:

"Do not be afraid, Daniel. Since the first day that you set your mind to gain understanding and to humble yourself before your God, your words were heard, and I have come in response to them." Daniel 10:12

Humility and understanding seem to go hand in hand. For those of us who grew up with the Schoolhouse Rock mantra "it's great to learn, 'cause knowledge is power", it can be tempting to think that information = understanding = power. In reality, what we seem to see in the bible is that humility is the beginning of understanding, and that acting on our understanding leads to wisdom. If we want to know God, it all begins with the act of humbling ourselves.

I am challenged to develop the discipline of humble seeking and to apply the discipline of "humbling myself" to my relationships and my work. Sure beats eating "humble pie."
