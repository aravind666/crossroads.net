Monday, December 5

The Invitation of Peace

PRAY

Ask God to reveal his peace in ways youve never known.

READ

Luke 2:34-35

REFLECT

In this part of Luke 2, Simeon holds baby Jesus and tells Mary that things are about to get ugly. His description sounds less like a day spa and a lot more like Jesus is going to wield some incredible power and disrupt a thing or two.

Years after Simeons prophesy, Jesus says to his close band of followers: "I have told you these things so that you may have peace. In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world" (John 16:33). In these words, we see the kind of peace Jesus brings. It isnt the tranquil and calm state we often associate with peace, and it isnt the avoidance of problems. Peace comes as we flourish in the midst of our trouble, because of our relationship with Him.

RESPOND

Have you ever cut anything out of your life, and although it may have been hard or painful, you can now see the relief and freedom it brought you? Thank God for the peace he gave you.
