Last week, we took my 16-month daughter to Cincinnati Children’s Hospital to have a cyst removed from around one of her eyes. It was a precautionary procedure to prevent the cyst from causing any future issues to her eye nerve. As a first-time parent, this was an extremely scary experience seeing our daughter go under anesthesia for the first time. 

 

Fortunately, everything went as smoothly as possible, and Lydia was back to her playful self, acting like nothing even happened only a couple days later (including diving off the couch to her daddy). As my wife and I were sitting at home thinking about how thankful we were for the amazing care at Children’s, what hit me the most after this experience was the thought that there are thousands of kids in South Africa (too many that I’ve played with) who could never receive a needed surgery like this...in fact, I’ve met too many kids that don’t have the basic necessities of nutrition and hygiene, much less health care. 

 

This observation has always prompted questions like "How can things be this way?" and "What went wrong with God’s plan?" or "How could He let things like this happen?" However, I think those are the wrong questions to be asking. Instead, I want to challenge myself and others to ask, "What am I doing about it? How am I being a part of God’s plan?"

 

Do I believe that poverty will be eradicated in South Africa overnight? No. Will everyone suddenty receive best-in-class healthcare? Probably not.  But I do believe that God can use a community like ours to start a literal revolution in South Africa, and hopefully give everyone involved an opportunity to be stretched and grow in new ways in the process. 

 

This is a huge and crazy task to take on, changing the face of medical care in some of the worst conditions. But the stakes are high in a region that is rampant with poverty and HIV, and that is why I couldn’t be more excited to see how God is going to work through this campaign to make the impossible happen in the years to come.
