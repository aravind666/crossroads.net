Wednesday, December 9
An Unusual Way of Love

Take a moment to pray and ask God to meet you during this time.


READ
Ephesians 5:21-6:9

REFLECT
Submission tends to be a very bad word in our culture. For most of our society, submission implies weakness, helplessness, and a general lack of other options. Submission is surrender, something to be done when all other options have failed.

But how does God see it?

All of the situations we read about today (marriage, parents, slaves and masters) are demonstrations of the idea of submission, and they are all connected to ways that Jesus has already submitted himself for our sake.

You could describe God in many ways, but certainly not as weak or helpless. So if Jesus willingly put aside his own comfort, pleasure, desires, power and position for our sakein other words, submitted on our behalfthen submission means something different entirely. Submission is the ultimate act of love.

  • What is your initial, gut-level response to the word submission?
  • How do we balance submission with not allowing people to manipulate or run us over?
  • How do you usually show love to others? In what ways are these acts of submission?

RESPOND
Spend a couple of minutes asking God to whom he wants you to submit to today. Once He brings someone to your mind, make a plan for how you are going to submit to him or her out of love today. It could be a coworker (possibly one you dont like) that needs help on a project, it could be apologizing first in a long running argument, or it could be something else entirely. The important thing is not to sit on it. Act on whatever plan you make as soon as possible.
