Shwana, a single mother whose child has learning issues, struggles to earn enough money to provide her son with the care he needs while she looks for a better-paying job. Shes is navigating a fragmented system of care in Cincinnati and is getting lost in the shuffle. CityLink Center is designed to help families like Shwanas, offering an integrated approach that combines relationships and excellent services that give them the best opportunity to move from poverty toward self-sufficiency.

CityLinks approach is part of a growing national trend to integrate services: The Annie E. Casey Foundation created the Centers for Working Families to encourage organizations to leverage their existing services in a smarter, more efficient approach through bundling assistance. And nationally, more than 30 organizations are utilizing this style of programming with promising results. Studies have shown that families receiving bundled services were 3-4 times more likely to achieve major economic outcomes than clients receiving only one type of service.
