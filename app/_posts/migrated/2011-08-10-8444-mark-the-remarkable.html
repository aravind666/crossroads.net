Wednesday, August 10

Mark the Remarkable

God, remind me of your power, presence and goodness in my life.

READ

Joshua 4:1-7

REFLECT

God called Joshua and the Israelites to do something remarkable, even impossible. Basically, God told them to "take the Promised Land from the big scary giant people. To get there, cross the Jordan River, which happens to be at flood stage. When you step into the water, the river will stop flowing and you will cross on dry land. Do exactly what I say. Trust me." And they did. And God showed up, just like he said he would.

After God showed up in a big way, he led the Israelites through the process of setting up a memorial. Even today, God wants his miracles known and celebrated. He wants his story told. And notice how He wants to be involved not just in how the miracles happen, but also in how they are celebrated.

RESPOND

Consider a time in your life when God did something remarkable. Maybe He healed a loved one, or gave you a child you thought you couldnt have. Maybe He asked you to do something terrifying, but you did it anyway. Today thank Him for that miracle, ask him what kind of memorial he would like for the miracle, and then create it. Maybe plant a tree, or make a garden stone as a family. It doesnt have to be big or fancy, just something that sparks the re-telling of the story - "This is to remember the day that God did this in my life."
