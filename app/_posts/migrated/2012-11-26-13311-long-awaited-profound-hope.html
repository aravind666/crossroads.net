Long Awaited: Profound Hope

This week weve looked at Old Testament prophesies that gave hope of a coming Messiah. Today lets look at two New Testament verses that teach how to continue to live in hope.

READ

2 Corinthians 4:18

Romans 8:24-25

REFLECT

Do you ever place your hope in all of the things you could make happen? (Like: I could work really hard and get that promotion, or I can make this relationship work.) Thats not hope, its worldly optimism. This Christmas season lets plant a seed of hope, water it and let it take root. Not hope that you will get a bike or an ipad for Christmas, but hope in Jesus and His Kingdom.

RESPOND

If the world waters our greedy hopes everyday with ads and upgrades, make a list today of things you could do to intentionally water a real seed of hope.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

You can use these questions to spark conversation with people who also do The Daily.

  • What do you think it would have felt like to be a part of the Jewish nation in the Old Testament and to continue to hear of this coming Messiah?
  • What is the difference between hope and worldly optimism?
  • Where in your life could you use more hope? Pray together to receive it.
