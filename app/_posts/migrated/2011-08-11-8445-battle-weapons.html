Thursday, August 11

Battle Weapons

God, please show me the power of my prayers.

READ

Joshua 6:1-21

REFLECT

Just after they crossed the Jordan River, the first city the Israelites had to conquer was the powerful city of Jericho. Standard military tactics would probably call for the Israelites to lay siege to the city. But once again, God shows us that his ways are not our ways. God chose to win this battle through the weapons of prayer and worship, not swords and shields. He instructed Joshua to fight this one differently, while also promising him that the battle had already been won.

When were faced with challenges, we can be tempted to think we should always be doing something. This can be overwhelming, and we dont know where to start. God offers us battle weapons that are far mightier - prayer and worship.

RESPOND

Today, face your challenges from a context of worshipping God. As you pray about your obstacles, instead of asking God what you should do, just worship. Tell God how awesome he is, play some worship music, whatever feels like worship for you. As you "march and blow the trumpets," trust that God will "topple the walls."
