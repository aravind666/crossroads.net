Tuesday, April 12

Floods to Freedom

God, thank you for the freedom you bring.

READ

Genesis 6:11-22

REFLECT

Imagine Noahs position in this one. First, he must have had to explain himself to the neighborsWhy the massive boat in a dry backyard? Why the zoo? And maybe further explain how God told him about the complete destruction headed their way. It must have sounded crazy, even offensive.

But Noah put himself in a position to hear God. He absorbed Gods instructions. Then he began buildingan aggressive act of faith. If there was ridicule from the community, he pushed through it. If there was personal doubt, he pushed through it. Noah trusted God would show him freedom, and God didin a way that didnt stop with Noah. We, too, experience the fruit of his faith.

RESPOND

Maybe it isnt building a gigantic boat, but there are definitely times when God asks things of us that appear crazy or too hard. (Maybe your Free Journey experience felt like a big movement of faith.) Yet, we have to keep our eye on the bigger story: God is healing, changing and freeing this world, and we get to be a part of it. Take a minute to thank God for letting you be part of his Kingdom at work.

READ THROUGH THE NEW TESTAMENT
