Thursday, October 6

Moving by Small Steps

Ask God to help you step out and trust him today.

READ

Philippians 4:19-20

REFLECT

In 1834, a 28-year-old pastor in Bristol, England founded an organization called the "Scripture Knowledge Institute" to train and support missionaries, distribute Bibles, and feed, house, and "scripturally educate" orphan children. Over the next 68 years, George Mueller would build five large orphan houses and care for over 10,000 orphans...all while pastoring a church and preaching three times a week.

Heres the crazy part of this whole story: over 68 years and 10,000 orphans, but not once did he ask for financial support. There was no government aid, no commercial appeals, no fundraising plan. Whenever they needed something-food, money, clothes, whatever it was-he prayed for it.

RESPOND

That story is very cool, but it can also sound very intimidating. Yet the thing we need to realize is the same thing that George Mueller learned early on. The kind of life we have been talking about these past few weeks-this radical movement into trusting God-doesnt come in a huge event. It is a long series of small choices made each day. These small choices increase our capacity (faith) to follow Gods voice and move in his direction.

Take a few minutes and ask God what ONE way he wants you to trust him today. Then move.
