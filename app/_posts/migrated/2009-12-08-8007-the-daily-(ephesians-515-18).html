Tuesday, December 8
True Wisdom

Take a moment to pray and ask God to meet you during this time.

READ
Ephesians 5:15-18

REFLECT
Wisdom. Its a word that might conjure up images of white-haired old guys doling out fortune cookie-type statements (think Mr. Miyagi or the guy from the yellowbook.com commercials). Wisdom is supposed to be deep, meaningful, meditative and serene, right? In other words, it feels like having a real life with a real schedule and real problems puts wisdom out of our reach.Yet Paul indicates something entirely different. He doesnt really offer much that we would consider deep or thoughtful, just encouragement to make the best use possible of our time, and to be filled with Gods Spirit. Paul is calling us to be strategic about the way we live our lives.
What might this kind of wisdom mean
for:

  • The way you use your free time?
  • The way you approach your relationships?
  • Your outlook on your job?

RESPOND
While its important to be strategic about our lives, we must not forget that the
foundation of true wisdom is our connection with Gods Spirit. In fact, it says
in James 1:5 that if we need wisdom, we should ask God and he will give it to
us. Take the rest of your time with God today to ask him to make you wise in
the various areas of your life. If He brings things to your mind, write them
down in your journal.
