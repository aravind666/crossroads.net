Thursday, November 29

Long Awaited: The Promise

This week, we are looking at the hope that was building through the Old Testament, and the way that Christ still gives hope today.

READ

Isaiah 9:6-7

REFLECT

Certain sects of the Jewish faith had put their hope in the Messiah being a warrior, assuming that he would deliver the long-time-persecuted-oppressed, and currently under Roman rule,Jewish people. If you have ever felt bullied or put down, its only human to want a superhero to come and kick some butt. Yet, Gods promised Messiah just didnt look like that. Even though its true that all authority and government really does rest on His shoulders (Romans 13), Jesus came to initiate a different kind of kingdom that begins in the hearts of men and women, and comes to fruition at Jesus return.

RESPOND

Think of something you have been hoping for: a new job, a spouse, or a whatever. Now picture handing that over to Jesus. Put your hope in Him.
