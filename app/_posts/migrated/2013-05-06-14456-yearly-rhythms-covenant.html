Yearly Rhythms: Covenant

We continue with our look at four topics that God wanted celebrated yearly through holidays he gave his people in the Old Testament.

READ

Leviticus 23:15-16

REFLECT

After Passover, when God delivered the Israelites out of Egypt, there were 50 days that passed before Moses headed up the mountain and brought down the law. This period of time is considered holy, and celebrated as the festival of weeks. It ends with Shavuot or Pentecost in Greek.

Shavuot celebrates the covenant God made with his people through the laws he gave to train freedom to former slaves. 1,500 years later, God would form a new covenant with his disciples on this day by writing the law on their hearts through the giving of the Holy Spirit.

RESPOND

Journal about how both the law of the Ten Commandments and the Holy Spirit are similar in what they offer.
