Tuesday, December 15
Waiting for a Promise

Take a moment to pray and askGod to meet you during this time.

READ
Micah 5:2-5

REFLECT
Before Jesus was born, God made many promises about how hewould send a saviorsomeone who would make things right between God and peopleonce and for all.

But since there was a lot of waiting that had to happen(and people, in general, arent very good at waiting), God sent messengers todescribe some of the signs that would identify this savior. Micah describes one of those signs herethat the Saviorwould be born in Bethlehem. The backwater, nowhere village of Bethlehem.

But in the meantime, they had to wait.

And wait.

Sound familiar?

  • In whatareas do you have difficulty waiting on Gods timing?
  • At theend of the passage, Micah says that when the Savior comes, He will be ourpeace. What does that mean to you.

RESPOND
Sometime today, go somewhere quiet where you can beundisturbed for five minutes or so. Ask God to show you areas where you need towait on his timing. As He brings things to mind, write them down in yourjournal. At the end of five minutes, ask Him to help you learn to wait on him inthese areas.
