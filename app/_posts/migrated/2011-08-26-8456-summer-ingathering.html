Friday, August 26

Summer: Ingathering

God, thank you for giving me the ability to work. May you also teach me to rest.

READ

Exodus 20:11, II Timothy 2:6

REFLECT

Your soul is at risk when you forget to rest and let go. No exaggeration. In our American culture, we tend to think we can (and need to) do it all. We worry that everything will fall apart without us--that we are so very, very important. We are, indeed, priceless. But only because the Creator of the universe is planted in us. We cant earn that value. We must pause, and revel in it. We must rest.

Rest doesnt necessarily mean lying on the couch, eating Cheetos, and watching TV. It might. But it might also mean getting out and moving around in what God has made. Resting in the Lord means not controlling, but receiving. Take a day to enjoy God.

RESPOND

Plan a Sabbath. Schedule it and decide what you want it to look like. If it doesnt seem fun enough to motivate you to do it, re-plan a day that will.

READ THROUGH THE NEW TESTAMENT

Todays reading is: 1 Corinthians 9

Week Two Discussion Questions

The Friday edition of The Daily features discussion questions based on the weeks content. These questions are meant to spark conversation with your spouse, your roommate, or a small group of friends who also subscribe to The Daily.

Summer is a season to try loving through serving and moving. Its also a season to tend to your inner life. A great way to really grow is to do it in community, and discuss that inner stuff. Inviting people into your inner life is a good discipline to form in the summer season.

  • Share something that you would really rather not. Whats something you dont want folks to know about you? Think back to the rocks.
  • Talk about your weeds. What are you struggling to release or add to your life?
  • Talk about Sabbath: Is it happening for you or not. Where could you start?
  • Have you considered what season you might be in? Still in spring, reveling in a newness with God? Or maybe summer, as you have put your love into action for the Kingdom? Discuss.
