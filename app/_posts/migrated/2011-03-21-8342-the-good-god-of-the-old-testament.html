Week 1: The Good God of the Old Testament

Monday, March 21

The Good God of the Old Testament

God, fill my heart and mind today with your abundant love and grace.

READ

James 1:17

REFLECT

Some of us have a tendency to imagine a killer God of the Old Testamenta god who says that if we do what he wants, well be okay; if we dont, hell make our lives miserable. But a view like this short-circuits our understanding of a God who isand who he always has beenthe source of knock-down, amazing love and grace. So well spend two weeks (with a special week in-between) reading and considering parts of the Old Testament and finding these layers of Gods character that we might sometimes overlook (or relegate to the New Testament).

RESPOND

Think of the last book you read, fiction or non-fiction. Now consider the main character. If the book is even remotely well-written, that character has complexities. You dont know everything about that character on page 1 or even 99. And Gods depth blows that characters depth out to the water. Spend a minute asking God to help you break free from any boxes youve put around him.

READ THROUGH THE NEW TESTAMENT
