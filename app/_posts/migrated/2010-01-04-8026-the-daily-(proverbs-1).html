Monday, January 4
Wisdom Defined

Spend a minute asking God to help keep your mind and heart open to the things he wants to teach you.

READ
Proverbs 1

REFLECT
When a new year rolls in, its a good time to look back over our priorities. Whether those priorities are personal, professional or spiritual, asking for and receiving Gods wisdom will lead to better decisions and a long list of benefits well read about this week in the book of Proverbs as we begin a new series on wisdom.

Proverbs 1:7 tells us, The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge. However, that brings up some questions like, What is the fear of the Lord? and How is it connected to wisdom?

Thankfully, fearing God doesnt mean living in constant terror of being punished by a cosmic bully. It means submitting to Him as our Creator and seeking his guidance as we make choices. We grow in wisdom as we grow in relationship with God, and as we respond to what hes teaching us by acting on those things in even small ways in our lives.

RESPOND
Write down two or three areas where you need wisdom right now, and keep the list somewhere you can find it again. Then during the next three weeks, keep a log of any Bible passages you read that are relevant to the areas you identified.
