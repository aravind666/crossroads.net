Friday, May 10

Yearly Rhythms: Home

Today we look at the last of four key holidays God gave his people in the Old Testament.

READ

Leviticus 23:41-43

Hebrews 11:8-10

REFLECT

Sukkot is a yearly holiday that can not go unnoticed in Jerusalem or Jewish communities that celebrate it. Everyone builds a temporary dwelling in front of his or her house and lives there for the week. It is a holiday meant to remember that the Israelites lived in temporary dwellings for 40 years after leaving Egypt.

Abraham lived in tents longer than he needed to, just to train his children that this world is not their home. In celebrating Sukkot, you look back at your home and say to your children, "That house, that is not our permanent dwelling. It is training to point us to eternity."

RESPOND

Listen to this song while you journal about ways that you can have yearly rhythms that keep your heart planted in God, instead of the temporary things in our line of vision.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

You can use these questions to spark conversation with people who also do The Daily.

  • What stuck out to you this week as difficult or intriguing elements to include as yearly rhythms?

  • Why do you think God chose these topics that his people return to once per year: deliverance, covenant, repentance and home?

  • Do you put any intentionality into the spiritual rhythms of your year? How could you be more intentional?
