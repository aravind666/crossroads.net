Kerry and Annie Hilton moved their family from New Zealand to Kolkata, India, to live among the poor. Kerry took a walk at night and realized they had moved into the largest red light district in India. They had not planned on putting themselves or their four children amidst a massive, active commercial sex industry. They easily could have chosen the “safe” route—to find a better neighborhood. But instead, they stayed and took the time to get to know their neighbors. Two years later, they met with a group of 20 women to begin what we know today as Freeset, a business for freedom.

 

The Hiltons created a company to primarily serve the needs of its employees, rather than select the best employees to produce a profit. With employment criteria being need-based instead of ability-based, they turned business on its head. The economics of what they produce is essential to their success, but their workers are not the most obvious choice of business partners.

 

Freeset is the Hiltons’ solution to giving women a viable job option to the sex trade. They identified the problem as economic and began to create an alternative. A community formed as the Hiltons invited women to build a business that produces export-quality custom bags and organic cotton t-shirts. Now 200 women are working for wages at the industry standard, with a pension, child care and health care. Dignity and freedom come with the job, making Freeset the ultimate package.

 

With 10,000 sex workers in a few square miles in Kolkata, Freeset has already given two percent of the women another way of life. This two percent is working in a sustainable industry and learning valuable skills. Although Freeset will continue to grow, it will not be able to accommodate 10,000. In response, the Hiltons have created a replicable model others can use to start businesses in India.

 

They are now building into entrepreneurs who will expand the scope of job opportunities for other at-risk populations. They are sharing what they have developed over the past 13 years to spark widespread, long-term, kingdom-inspired freedom through the “Freeset Incubator.”

 

The Hiltons were recently honored at the National Underground Railroad Freedom Center as “Everyday Freedom Heroes” for their outstanding work and commitment to freedom. Fair trade business not only offers a choice to employees but to consumers. As they choose daily how to manage their business, we can consider the impact of our everyday choices.
