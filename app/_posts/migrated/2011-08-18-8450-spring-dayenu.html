Thursday, August 18

Spring: Dayenu

God, you have already done so much for me. And Im thankful.

READ

1 Thessalonians 5:16-18

REFLECT

There is a traditional Hebrew word used in Passover meals: Dayenu, which means,"it would have been enough and Im thankful." When we are in the spring season with God, it is a good time to form a rhythm of gratitude-a habit that can carry on into the other seasons of the year. Thankfulness is the greatest weapon we have against greed, selfishness, entitlement, depression and worry.

Giving thanks is easiest during this season, when we are so close to experiencing Gods new love, a new awe of him, and his rescue and redemption.

The Israelites said "Dayenu" in response to the Lord rescuing them out of Egypt, feeding them in the desert, and giving them the holy day of Passover. We can create our own list of Dayenu: it would have been enough and Im thankful.

RESPOND

Write a thankfulness list to God. Think through your life up to present day, writing things for which you are thankful. Try out the word "Dayenu," or write out: It would have been enough and Im thankful.
