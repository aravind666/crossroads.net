After her mother sent hert to live a life of bondage in the brothels of Sonagachi, Samudri* was rescued by local law enforcement earlier this year with the help of International Justice Mission (IJM)’s offices in Mumbai and Kolkata.

 

Since then, Samudri has been in the care of an IJM partner aftercare home. For months, Crossroads volunteers have been praying alongside IJM for Samudri as IJM social workers engaged with her, giving her love, encouragement and support, despite her negative attitude and dejected disposition.

 

Recently, an IJM social worker shared a breakthrough with the rest of the IJM-Mumbai team: Samudri has been formally enrolled in the 10th standard English medium and a joyful spirit has renewed her! The superintendent of the home commended Samudri in front of our social worker, saying that she was not only making progress in her studies but that she also was excelling at them! Imagine the smile on Samudri’s face upon hearing such praise. God is performing miracles in our midst and it is amazing to see the lives of these young women changing before our very eyes.

 

This is why we are dedicated to creating quality aftercare in India, so that we can have many other answered prayers like Samudri. Please continue to keep her and all of the girls who are (or were) victims of forced prostitution as we work to rescue them and then help them build new lives.

 

*Pseudonyms are used to protect the victim’s identity.
