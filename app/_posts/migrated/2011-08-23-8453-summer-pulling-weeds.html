Tuesday, August 23

Summer: Pulling Weeds

God, may you make my heart a fertile place where your Spirit, your Word and your desires take root.

READ

Mark 4:2-8

REFLECT

This is a good time to remember that entering a summer phase in our faith does not mean that we leave the learnings of spring behind. If we move into doing, yet we have bad or untended inner soil, the goodness of summer cant take root.

Weeds hang out in plain sight above the surface. They can be people or systems in our life that choke out time or a desire for God. You can try to will a plant to grow, but the truth is that our relationship--like a plant--will grow naturally if we set ourselves before God, allowing him to dig up rocks and show us what weeds should be pulled from our lives. The soil of our heart stays soft when we remain humble and thankful.

RESPOND

Today, lets deal with the weeds. Ask God to show you places in your life where there seems to be distraction or blockage. Whats choking out your desire or time for Him? Maybe its how you entertain yourself or spend your time. Or maybe its who you choose to be with. Write down your answers. Take a step and remove a weed.
