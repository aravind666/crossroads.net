Thursday, April 19

Acts: Before the Sanhedrin

Reminder: this weeks study is about the Apostle Paul, an early follower of Jesus who "changed the game." The study is presented in a paraphrase of the Bible called The Message, which gives us biblical text in simple, modern language.

READ

Acts 22:30-23:11

REFLECT

Paul endured many hardships during his ministry, including this confrontation with the Sanhedrin, a council of the Jews ruling religious leaders and supreme court. Paul was always bold (and in this case, a littletoobold with the man he didnt recognize as the Chief Priest). He was also strategic, here appealing to one faction of the Sanhedrin, the Pharisees, and highlighting the major theological difference that separated them from their counterparts on the council. Ultimately, though, Paul relied not on his own boldness or quick wit, but on the will of his master, Jesus, who appeared to encourage Paul that hed soon be out of trouble and on to Rome.

RESPOND

Is there anywhere in your life that you feel persecuted or "on trial?" Take a moment now to ask God to come to your rescue, and trust him with the outcome.
