Week One: The Power of the Tongue

Monday, July 18

A Matter of Life and Death

God, teach me about the power of the tongue.

READ

Proverbs 18:21

REFLECT

Its possible that the originator of the infamous nursery rhyme "Sticks and Stones" had the best of intentions. However, this little saying has been a twisted source of one of the biggest lies ever spoken. Not only can "words hurt," the Bible says that they carry the power to speak life or speak death.

Over the next two weeks, well look at where these powerful words come from: the tongue. The Bible has much to say about this mysterious little muscle, with all its power and tricks. Well consider questions such as, "What should my tongue be producing? When should it be still? And why does it like the taste of my foot so much?"

RESPOND

Has anyone ever hurt your feelings with words? Did you try to seek comfort in the nursery rhyme, "Sticks and Stones?" Or have you ever used that rhyme to justify hurting someone else? Spend some time today considering the truth that words have real power. Write down your thoughts.
