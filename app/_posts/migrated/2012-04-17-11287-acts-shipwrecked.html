Acts: Shipwrecked

Reminder: this weeks study is about the Apostle Paul, an early follower of Jesus who "changed the game." The study is presented in a paraphrase of the Bible called The Message, which gives us biblical text in simple, modern language.

READ
Acts 27:13-15, 27:21-26, 27:39-28:10

REFLECT
Paul encountered and was dramatically converted by the resurrected Christ. He went on to spend the rest of his life sharing the Gospel and building and encouraging churches all over the ancient world. So its probably pretty safe to say that Paul spent a great deal of his time exactly where God wanted him, following the direction of the Holy Spirit and fulfilling Gods will for his life.

And yet, Paul was no stranger to hardship and distress.He even boasted of his many sufferings, knowing that the hardships he endured would draw him closer to God and make him more like Jesus. And Paul relied on God for everything, knowing that he would be delivered from his troubles, thatGod would work in the midst of hardship to bring about good things, and that, ultimately,Gods grace in his life was sufficient.

RESPOND
Ask God to transform your perspective on any trouble youre enduring. Ask Him to sustain you, teach you and show you his power in the midst of it. Trust that He will respond to you and save you, just as he did for Paul.


DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
You can use these questions to spark conversation with people who also read The Daily. This week, discuss your answers to some of the questions youve wrestled with each day this week.
  • How have you been tempted to take credit for Gods work in your life?
  • Have you discovered any "idols" in your life that have consumed too much of your time and attention (your "worship")?
  • Is there anywhere in your life that you feel persecuted or "on trial?"
  • How are you suffering right now, and what are you learning in the midst of it?
