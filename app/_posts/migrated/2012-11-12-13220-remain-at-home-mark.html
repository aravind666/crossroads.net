Thursday, November 15

Remain at Home: Mark

Mark is a bold man who leads his wife and family without fear. When asked how he experiences God as a dad, he shared a poem; a modern day Psalm. Here it is.

(If you missed this weekends message, watch a video summaryhere.)

MEET MARK

"Once I was a man without a home

A wanderer devoid of wonder

In a perpetual state of thirst

My heart radiating loneliness

Once, but no longer.

Now I sing "How lovely is your dwelling place..."I am a son of the King.

Be still & understand.

Let the trumpets skip atop the mountains

I shout joyfully to the Living God.

I sing Your praise always and forever.

The Living God is my home, my rock, my shield.

Beat your hands on the drum

The redemptive dance, it has begun!

My home is in in the New Jerusalem

And the Lord Jesus, Faithful - my brother.

Heaven is my home.

Where the Creators breath blows freely.

I was lost. He found me.

I was dirty. He renewed me.

I was guilty. He redeemed me.

My home is the hand of my Abba.

I am free to live, serve, love with no compromises.

Selah.

The Lord Jesus made my home with Him,

Now I am free to live and love. Amen."

REFLECT

Marks poem sounds like a Psalm, which were songs or poems mainly written by David in the Bible.Psalm 91:1-4is a Psalm that shows Gods protection over us.
