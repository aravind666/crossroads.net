 You may have heard stories and seen pictures from Mumbai, India, where Crossroads has been partnering with International Justice Mission  (IJM) for several years to rescue and rehabilitate victims of forced prostitution. But as a part of the Game Change campaign, we’re broadening our efforts to include Kolkata,  another Indian city which is sometimes called Calcutta (similar to how Mumbai is often called Bombay). 

 

For three-and-a- half years, I led the IJM field office to combat sex trafficking of children in Calcutta (see previous blog) before coming on staff with Crossroads.  Of note, 42% of the Indian girls whom IJM has rescued in Mumbai are from West Bengal, the state in which Calcutta is the capital.  Here is a link to a news piece on an investigative, undercover video negotiating prices for 11-year-old girls in Calcutta.  (The CNN video begins with a short advertisement, then two short clips follow.)

 

These stories represent thousands of women and girls in the flesh trade in Songachi, India’s largest red-light area. These stories represent the girls we are going to rescue through the Game Change campaign.
