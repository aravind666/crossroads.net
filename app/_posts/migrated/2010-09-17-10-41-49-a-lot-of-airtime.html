Rock star Bono, in his address at the National Prayer Breakfast, called out that “it’s not a coincidence that in the Scriptures, poverty is mentioned more than 2100 times….that’s a lot of airtime…” 

 

In Richard Stearns’ recent book, The Hole in Our Gospel, the World Vision CEO points readers to a passage in the Bible (Luke 4) where Jesus unrolls the scroll of the prophet Isaiah and states that “the Spirit of the Lord is on me, because he has anointed me to preach good news to the poor. He has sent me to proclaim freedom for the prisoners and recovery of sight for the blind, to release the oppressed, to proclaim the year of the Lord’s favor.” Isn’t it interesting that the first recipients of this gospel are the poor? Stearns challenges readers with this question: What has the church done to bring good news to the world’s three billion poor?  

 

Throughout the Bible, Jesus showed us how to tangibly love the poor by his example of healing the sick, feeding the hungry, and clothing the naked. Perhaps at Crossroads, you’ve demonstrated love for the poor in our city by participating in Angel Tree, Go Cincinnati, Thanksgiving Food Drive, or Whiz Kids. But there is so much more to be done.

 

Just yesterday, the US Census Bureau released its annual report on income, poverty and health insurance in the US. The latest poverty rate in America is 14.3%, which means that there are more people in poverty in America than ever before.  That’s one in seven, or 44 million Americans, who are living in poverty. the highest number of people in poverty since the government began keeping track in 1959.
