All Ye Faithful: The Shepherds

When the birth of Jesus was announced, Mary and Joseph didnt send a photo of the baby to family or friends. God announced it to an unexpected group of shepherds.

LISTEN

O, Come All Ye Faithful

READ

Luke 2:8-14

REFLECT

Shepherds were the most unlikely people for God to announce the birth of the Savior. Society considered them unclean, physically and spiritually. Spending day after day, night after night in the fields herding sheep was a dirty job. They were outcasts.

While the rest of Bethlehem slept, these shepherds lay awake watching faithfully over their flock. The stars twinkled silently over them and breezes rushed through the grasses. The shepherds expected this to be a quiet and routine night. Maybe a stray sheep at some point, but nothing out of the ordinary. So they watched and waited for sunrise.

RESPOND

God announced the birth of his Son to an unlikely, dirty group. This "great news of great joy" is for all the people. From the kings right down to the shepherds. And you. Spend some time taking in that Jesus saves all people.
