Acts: Know Your Audience

The Daily continues to follow the book of Acts and how we can apply its lessons to our own lives.

PRAY

Ask God to help you understand the people of influence in your life.

READ

Acts 22:22-28

REFLECT

Paul did what a lot of us do: he said some really personal things to the wrong people. While he knew the crowd was angry, and no matter what he said theyd probably be upset, he hoped his personal story would make them come around. They didnt. They had him arrested without a fair trial. But Paul found an audience in the Roman guards who were supposed to beat him. When they found out he was a Roman citizen by birth, they decided to at least give him the trial he deserved.

RESPOND

How would you share your personal story with two different audiences, like a family member and a co-worker? Think about how you would tell it based on who they are, what they already know about you and what you know about them. Journal about it or if you feel a nudge to tell your story, go and do it.
