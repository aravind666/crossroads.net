Friday, December 25
An Inconvenient Birth

Take a moment to pray and ask God to meet you during this time.

READ
Luke 2:1-7

REFLECT
It really was an inconvenient birth. Jesus came to a dad who wasnt expecting a son and a mom who wasnt expecting a baby at all. His parents were traveling, and the local hotel didnt have any vacancies. They ended up staying in the stable, where Jesus was placed in a feeding trough after his birth. And it almost seemed fitting, since his own nation missed it, foreigners had to search for him, and shepherds were the best PR available.

Youd think God would have a more polished operation.

Unless that was the point all along. None of it makes sense, unless God was telling us that wed have to search, that we should expect some inconvenience, and that there is far more to the story than what we first see.

  • Have you ever felt like God has messed up your plan? What happened as a result?

RESPOND
It can be difficult to remember that sometimes what feels like inconvenience is part of Gods plan for restoration. Take a few minutes today and write down some areas where God may want to bring his restoration into your life (even if that might feel like hes messing up your plan). Ask Him to help you trust him in these areas.

Week 2 Discussion Questions

The Friday edition of The Daily features discussion questions based on the weeks content. These questions are meant to spark conversation with your spouse, your roommate, or a small group of friends who also subscribe to The Daily.

Its safe to say that the first Christmas was not what anyone expected. Following Jesus has a way of messing up some of our well-ordered plans.

  • How has His offer of redemption and restoration changed your life? Your relationships? Your goals?
  • What are some practical ways you deal (or could deal) with the tension of trusting God when you cant see the whole plan?

If we look at the assortment of people who showed up to celebrate the birth of Jesus, we see that there is a place for everyone.

  • What does this say to you about the character of God?
  • What character from the Christmas story do you most identify with? Why?
  • How have you felt acceptance from God this Christmas?
