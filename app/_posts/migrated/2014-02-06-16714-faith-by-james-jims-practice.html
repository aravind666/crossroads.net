Faith by James: Jims Practice

Jims practice is ongoing, but he has chosen to suffer well, leaning in to a Father who loves him.

PRAY

Ask God to train you for the life he has planned.

READ

James 1:1-12

JIMS PRACTICE

I have been a smoker for the last 15 years and I have spent more years smoking than I have not. That is tragic when you think about it. Regardless, I am at a place in my life where I believe I need to change. Maybe God has softened my heart, maybe it is just time or maybe my priorities look different, but the most important part for me is having a desire to change.

Ive tried to quit smoking before. Usually at a point when I felt like I failed or everything in my life was out of control. I would say to myself, Just fix this one thing and then you can get right again. The problem is I am broken. It has taken me some time to learn this, but I know that by myself I am inadequate. I try giving it my best effort and fail.

Over the past year or so I have found that in my failure there is something I am missing. If quitting is about me and my abilities, I am not strong enough to beat this by myself. Stated differently, I have no track record for success when it comes to discipline in this area of my life and a lot of others if I am honest. If I want this to be a personal fight between me and smoking, I know what will happen. But if I want to frame this challenge as a spiritual test it can look different. I can have hope.

Where does this leave me? I know I want to change. I know it is going to be hard for me, my wife, my coworkers and friends. I know that in my own abilities I am not up for the challenge. But God is in control and has the power to do something I cannot. So I am going to take some action steps and ask God to fill in where I cannot. I am going to stop buying cigarettes and stop acting like the next cigarette will be there. I am going to drop my pride down a few notches and tell God every time I want to smoke and hope that he will relieve my craving. I want to suffer through this so I know what it is to be obedient to God instead of being obedient to my desires. I want to be trained out of this. I want to treat my body like Gods temple. I dont know how this story will end, but I trust God will train me if I ask Him. I suppose that is all I really want in life---to have my Father hear me when I cry out to Him.

PRACTICE

If youre ready for something to change in your life, whats one simple action step you can take to make that happen? Like Jim, remember to bring God alongside your suffering.
