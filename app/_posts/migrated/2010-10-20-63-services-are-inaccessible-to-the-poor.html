One of the most compelling reasons behind the creation of CityLink Center is very clear: people without access to reliable transportation cannot possibly address all of the other areas of their life where they are lacking resources.

Have you ever spent most of the day running errands?

  • Go to Ikea and get the bookcase you saw online.
  • Pick up a prescription at the pharmacy.
  • Stock up at the grocery store (Youve got space in your pantry.).
  • Hit the gym and get in a quick workout.

Most likely, you zipped from place to place easily with resources to pay for each stop. At the end of the day, youre exhausted but happy that youve accomplished so many non-essential but still-have-to-get-done tasks. Life is now organized and you can kick back and relax.

But what if your to do list that day looked like this?

  • Drop off your tenth application in three years to the McDonalds closest to your home (Its two bus transfers away, but you havent worked in five years.).
  • Go to Super Jobs to ask again about the job you really want but dont have the training for (Youre hoping theyve changed the requirements).
  • Find a free babysitter so that you can attend a GED class at Cincinnati State (Youve missed three classes already, one more and youll have to start over.).
  • Borrow money from your mother who lives in Dayton, Ohio, so that you can take your rent to your landlord who lives in the East End.
  • Get the baby to the free clinic for the cough that wont go away.

Hard? No, impossible. Without a car or someone to take you place to place, its impossible to navigate this list of essential and must-be-done tasks in a day. The baby is sick and hungry, you have no money and no job. Everyone you know is in exactly the same situation, so there is no one to help you. Its so easy to give up.

At CityLink Center, crucial services like job training, GED classes, financial skill-building and health care will all happen under one roof. One stop. Thats possible. Thats hope. One by one, well help people build up resources while walking along side them through job coaching and mentoring. One by one, well break the cycle of generational poverty in families.

Are you in?
