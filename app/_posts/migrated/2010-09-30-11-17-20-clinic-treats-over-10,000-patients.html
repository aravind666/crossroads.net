As many of you know, we have been working with OneSight over the past two weeks to deliver world-class eye care to people in Mamelodi. Our goal was to see10,000 people over two weeks. 

 

Today, with one day still to go, we reached 10,700! That’s 10,700 people who received a full eye exam to rival anything one might receive from an eye doc here in Cincinnati, complete with prescription glasses and sunglasses. Most importantly, over a dozen people were diagnosed with some type of condition or disease that, left untreated, would most likely lead to blindness. They now have a great chance to keep their sight. Last week, we had a team of 17 people from Crossroads working alongside the OneSight team.

 

To give you an idea of what is happening, check out this video of the line that Jason shot this morning. When the team arrived at 7am, there were easily 1,000 people already in line, and some had arrived as early as 2am. This demonstrates how desperate the people of Mamelodi are for good eye care and how powerful something like this can be.

 

On Friday, Pastor Titus, our key partner in Mamelodi, told me that he has never had this many people come up to him and tell him that they cannot believe what is happening at Charity & Faith, that something this good is actually happening in the township. Titus declared that this is so big that no one in Mamelodi can ignore it and deny that God is moving in Mamelodi. In fact, this week, multiple government officials, who previously denied us assistance with the clinics, showed up with their families to have their own eyes checked. This is a huge win for what we’re all doing in South Africa.You can read more on OneSight’s blog.
