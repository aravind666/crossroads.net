As were developing the programming behind CityLink Center, were creating very specific definitions to clearly communicate the complicated issues were tackling. Some of the most important definitions involve the resources our clients already have along with the resources they lack.

We call the matrix that lists resources and the degree to which a person has them the need spectrum. In placing a client on the need spectrum, the key resource areas we focus on are:

  • Employment
  • Finances
  • Health
  • Housing
  • Relationships
  • Spiritual growth
  • Transportation

At one end of the spectrum are at-risk individuals. These are people with virtually no resources who lack housing, income, employment and a healthy community to support them. Often they also have physical and/or mental health issues.

At the other end of the spectrum are thriving individuals, or people who have all of the resources they need of success: They are employed full time in a job with benefits; are financially literate and working on money goals; own a home or are renting a non-subsidized/market rent apartment; are engaged in healthy community and a local church and have a car or funds to use public transportation.

In between these two ends of the spectrum is the area we call stabilization. A stablilizing person might be working part-time at a job with no benefits or future growth opportunities; living in a shelter or with a friend; and has enough money to eat today but not necessarily tomorrow. They are one lost job, one eviction and one sickness away from being labeled at-risk.

The key thing to remember about resources is that they complement each other. You need all of them at the same time to be able to utilize them. For example, if you have a car but no job and therefore no money to use and maintain it, youre not thriving. If you have a job but no transportation to get to it, youre not thriving.

The work of CityLink Center is to move our clients one by one from stabilizing to thriving status in every resource area, helping them move from poverty to self-sufficiency.
