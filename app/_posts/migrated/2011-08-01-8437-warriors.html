Week One: Lessons from Joshua

Monday, August 1

Warriors

God, teach me how to be a warrior for your Kingdom.

READ

Exodus 17:8-16

REFLECT

Moses is the general, Joshua is the field commander. To fight the battle, Moses spells out a simple strategy - "you fight, Ill pray." Joshua didnt question or doubt. He did exactly what he was told - fighting the battle with the sword, trusting that Moses was handling his end. And the battle was won.

For the next two weeks, well be looking at the life of Joshua, a warrior and leader in the Old Testament. Today, we can see that no matter how strong we are, were not meant to face our battles alone. We need a Moses praying for us as we fight. And likewise, we can be the Moses for others as they face their own challenges.

RESPOND

Consider and write about a battle you are currently facing. Do you have "a Moses on the hilltop" praying for you? If so, call that person today with gratitude and encouragement. If not, ask God to bring to mind someone whocouldpray for you as you fight. Call that person, and ask them to pray for you.
