Julie* is a part-time working mom with five children.  For many years, she has worked diligently as a home health aide to support her family.  This year, thanks to a mentoring relationship like the ones CityLink will offer, Julie and her family purchased their first home in Westwood! Here is her story:

 

Julie’s employment and housing situation are relatively stable, but she and her family face many of the same challenges that are common among families who have been in generational poverty. Financially, Julie’s salary coupled with the survivor benefits she received after losing her husband are not sufficient to meet all of her family’s needs. And, while she’d like to work more, Julie’s son has special needs that require extra time and attention.

 

There are numerous resource gaps that have limited Julie and her family. Firstly, she lacks a support network. When one of her children gets sick at school, she has no one to call for last-minute childcare. Secondly, bus transportation is not provided for her children’s school. Both of these resource gaps affect Julie’s ability to work. as much as she’d like. In addition, Julie has a learning disability, making reading and writing a challenge for her.  She’s doing her best to advocate for her family, particularly her son with special needs, but she is not always aware of the resources available to assist her.

 

Enter Julie’s mentor, Susan, who is helping to bridge these resource gaps by guiding Julie and connecting her with services that can help her family, such as a first-time home-buyer’s grant from Smart Money, and educational services to help Julie overcome her learning disability. Susan also has helped Julie with completing applications, finding a supportive church community and making financial decisions, including her home purchase. And, most importantly, Susan is part of a growing support network on which Julie can rely in times of need.

 

Julie is one of the many faces of generational poverty; a woman who is motivated to care for her family, but who lacks access to resources that will move her out of generational poverty. But with her mentor Susan walking alongside her, Julie purchased a home and is working on her nursing degree.

 

*Julie’s story is real, but her name was changed to protect her privacy.
