In 1995, a group of 11 Cincinnatians felt a need. They wanted a place in Hyde Park to meet with friends and explore questions about God without pretending they had it all together or wading through a bunch of religious lingo. They invited their friends. Their friends showed up. And theyre still showing up today.

As a community weve been given a unique role in the Kingdom of God. We speak a dialect that people whove given up on church but havent necessarily given up on God understand and respond to. And while our goal has always remained the same: to connect people seeking God with a community of growing Christ-followers (who are changing the world), our vision has expanded beyond Hyde Park and Mt Lookout.

Over the last 14 years, God has used Crossroads to bring thousands of people into a relationship with Jesus. And we dont think hes finished using us. The last census said there were approx. 330,000 folks whod call themselves Cincinnatians. As you can imagine, they wont all fit at 3500 Madison Rd. At the same time, the Lord is calling this community to go after some big things, things that are close to His heart, but will require a larger base of folks to accomplish. To passionately pursue everyday Cincinnatians we need spaces around the city, in their neighborhoods, that allow friends to invite friends into a place that welcomes their questions and speaks their language.

We need multi-site.
