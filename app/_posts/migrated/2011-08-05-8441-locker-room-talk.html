Friday, August 5

Locker Room Talk

God, instill in me your boldness and encouragement.

READ

Joshua 1:1-9

REFLECT

Modern cinema has provided us with some great locker room talks. Coach Dale in Hoosiers, Coach Boone in Remember the Titans-these guys inspire us, fire us up, bring a tear to our eye. But what about the coaches? Who fires them up and makes them cry?

In this passage, the new coach of the Israelites (perhaps the most rebellious and stubbornest team of all) is receiving his inspiration from The Coach, God Almighty. Imagine Joshua sitting alone in the locker room, preparing for his team, eyes and ears locked on his Coach. And God lays it on him: "No one will be able to stand up against you all the days of your life... Ill never forsake you... Be strong and courageous." Now, go take those Israelites across the Jordan River.

RESPOND

Not only did God give Joshua instruction, but he also encouraged him in the midst of it. Maybe Joshua was just like the rest of us-sometimes afraid, sometimes discouraged. But he believed God. Read the passage again, imagining that God is talking to you alone. Is there a "Jordan River" in your life that God is telling you to cross? Do you believe God and all of his promises? Or are you struggling with fear or doubt? Write about your answers. Repent for any fear and doubt, and tell God aloud that you choose to believe Him.
