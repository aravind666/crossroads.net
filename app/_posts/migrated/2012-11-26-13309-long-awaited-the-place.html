Wednesday, November 28

Long Awaited: The Place

We continue to walk through prophecy this week to see Jesus as the unexpected fulfillment of the hope being pointed toward in the Old Testament.

READ

Micah 5:2

REFLECT

There were prophecies in the Old Testament like this one that said that the Messiah would come through Bethlehem. There were also prophesies that said he would be called a Nazarene, and inHosea 11:1that God would call his son out of Egypt. In Matthew 2 (readhereif you want) it tells the story of how Jesus was born in Bethlehem due to the census, fled to Egypt to flee from Herod, and then was raised in Nazareth. He fulfilled all of the prophecies, even when it felt like they contradicted each other.

RESPOND

Can you think of a place where God met you that was unexpected-like you understood Him differently, had a paradigm shift, or experienced unusual joy? Journal about it.
